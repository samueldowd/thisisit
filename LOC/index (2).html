Research and Evaluation

EDUC-999

Professor Deborah Merriam

Fall 2013

Reflection

This was a great class to take later on in my program. In fact, when it came time for me to take this class I was very excited for it. I really enjoyed the process of exploring possible topics, writing a proposal, deciding on methods, conducting research, doing a review of literature, and finally compiling a full research paper. This was not a process that I had ever been through before, and I found it to be both exciting and even fun at times.

My research paper was a study on the effectiveness of presentation medium on student learning. I focused specifically on whether a video lecture was more effective than a written lecture in an online course. During this class I was working at McGraw-Hill Education as a Product Manager on a project that was focused on building an experimental online course platform. In that sense, this research was very valuable to my team and I since it gave us a perspective of what technologies helped students learn best.

The results of the study were inconclusive, as was much of the research I looked at. However, the fact that the learning outcomes were just about equal for video and text was relatively significant itself. Using video (or just online courses in general) as a means of delivering a lecture is a fairly new idea. The fact that students were able to capture and retain information to the same degree as they would with text was surprising. As our culture becomes more screen-oriented, and continues to consume content digitally rather than from tangible materials like a book or a packet of paper, the options for presentation media begin to increase significantly. Podcasts, animations, screencasts, and others are all other methods of presenting lectures. Education as a whole, particularly students, would benefit a lot from further, and more broad, study in this area.
