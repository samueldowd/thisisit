Impact of Technology on Education

INST-943

Professor Mary Ellen Dubois

Fall 2011

Reflection

This class was my first class that I took as part of the Educational Technology program. It was a perfect course to introduce me to the program and give me the basic knowledge and tools to be successful. This course, like several others, was not simply theoretical and conceptual. Almost all of the assignments in this class were relevant to the work I was doing, and able to be used with the teachers I was helping.

The class consisted of many lab activities and other more developmental assignments. The assignments were helpful in developing a basic understanding of what the lab activities would assess. This was a great way to learn the content, but also helped me understand a great way of teaching complex concepts to others. I used many of the artifacts in my job, like the Web Committee PowerPoint listed below. By using the techniques and strategies learned in the course I was far more successful than had I constructed something on my own.

One reason that this course was great to start off with was that it helped introduce me to lesson plans and how to brainstorm, outline, and then finally plan a lesson. It was great experience to create lesson plans, and then use them in a classroom. I was responsible for teaching a Freshman Technology Seminar and used one of the assignments from this course to develop a lesson plan for the class. This exercise helped me prepare for the class, and ensure that I was including the right components and had structure built into the lesson.

This class was excellent as it balanced the instruction of how to incorporate technology into instruction, as well as using that instruction in practical and real world situations. This was useful because it allowed me to create lessons or presentations in an iterative fashion, and gain feedback at verious checkpoints. My approach to teaching using technology, as well as teaching about technology was improved significantly through both learning via the text and discussions, but also through practice.
