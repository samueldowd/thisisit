The key here is in the word “system”. The product by itself doesn’t relaly do much. But when a patient is able to put the colors, timed reminders, and the overall setup together, it
can be very effective. The product involves one tray of
medicine for every day of the month. Each tray has a slot for morning, noon, evening, and night time medicines. The trays are filled up a month at a time and sit in a stacked stand where they are arranged by day of the month. Each tray is numbered on both ends. One end in red, the other in green. Upcoming days are in green, while days that have
passed are flipped over once empty and shown in red. There is a spot at the front of the product to put the tray for that particular day. There is also a digital calendar/clock that has 4 daily alarms corresponding to the slots in the trays. The device alerts the user visually on the calendar, verbally by announcing that it is time to take the medicine, and also by setting of an alarm. The device even has extra loud volume levels for people with hearing loss. By using colors, the clock/calendar, and a repeatable process, the MedCenter encourages medication adherence and does so very easily with minimal electronic gadgetry.
