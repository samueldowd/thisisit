Mathematics Instruction with Technology

INST-951

Professor Mary Ellen Dubois

Spring 2012

Reflection

After taking INST-943 with Mary Ellen, I was really looking forward to this class. I was looking forward to a similar experience to that which I experienced in the previous class. I was not disappointed. This class was equally captivating, and equally effective at helping me understand the possibilities for educational technology, as well as allowing me to practice their use.

There were a number of different assignments that focused on different themes related to the instruction of mathematics using technology. I really liked the concept of the mini-lesson. It was great practice for the larger lesson plans, and also provided great practice for developing smaller, and quicker lessons for classes. While I was not a math teacher at the time, I was able to work with several math teachers in the High School where I worked to implement several of the tools and tactics we learned. In particular, I was able to work with a Middle School teacher on developing a lesson for Mean, Median, and Mode that was very similar to the one I created for this class.

In particular I really enjoyed the unit about Problem Solving. We were able to come up with a topic that would help teach students problem solving skills. This skill, at the level I was interested in, was no longer just multiplication tables, and long division. I was able to develop a lesson that had students look at creating a budget at different income levels in the United States. Students had to create budgets for 3 different subsets of the U.S. population as determined by income level. This lesson was a great example for me of how to incorporate real-world learning with subjects that also correlated directly to state frameworks.
