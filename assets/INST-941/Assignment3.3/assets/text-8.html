Learning Objectives: 1. How to access and use the Harvard University Library system to enhance work efficiency and reliability. 2. The benefits of applying the Harvard University Library resources in the workplace as a Staff member 3. An understanding of resources that exist as part of the Harvard University Library system verses what resources might be available outside of the system to the average individual
Access to online library resources is something that all Harvard employees have, but few take
advantage of. A good place to introduce this would be during the new employee technology orientation which the Technology Consulting Services department conducts each week. As stated in the learning objectives, it would first be important to show the new employee how to access the online library resources. Take them through a step by step tutorial and actually show them a search in Academic Search premier to get them started. Next, focus on the benefits of using the library resources over just a simple Google search. It may take a little longer to find the right article or resource, but it is certainly more reliable and likely to please whomever the new staff member is reporting to. In fact, for faculty assistants it is a great way to find Harvard Business Review articles that their faculty member may have written in order to gain insight into the work of the faculty members they support. In addition, having these resources available means there should be no excuse to have incorrect information in a report or deliverable for your boss. By using the library resources one can ensure that they are using the best available information, as well as the most factual. Lastly, take the new employees through the list of resources and explain the features like InterLibrary Loan which will allow them to gain access to books at all of the Ivy League libraries. Additionally go through Academic Search Premier, the Harvard scholarly works repository, and how the separation of schools works with the libraries (essentially explaining that even though the work for the Business School they have access to the libraries at all schools).
This would provide a good summary and simply make the new employees aware of a resource
that many staff do not take advantage of. In addition to being a good source of reliable and accurate information, it could give new employees a secret tip or leg up on staff who have already been working at Harvard but forgotten about the library resources. Obviously if there were more time it would be nice to cover more details like what each database can do, how the Google Books search works, and some good search tips, but with the limited time, this lesson covers the most important parts. It would also be helpful to have a handout of some kind, or quick reference guide for the libraries that the new employee could take with them. It would have links to the library sites, explanations of the databases, information on InterLibrary Loan, library hours, etc. It would be a good resource as the new employee starts so that they can keep it close to their desk and reference it easily when necessary.
