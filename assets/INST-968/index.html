Introduction to Assistive Technology

INST-968

Professor Katherine Hibbard

Summer 2013

Reflection

I was apprehensive at the outset of this class. I did not have much experience with assistive technology, and particularly not in an educational setting. That said, I was eager to learn about the possibilities that existed and how they could be integrated in education to make a concerted effort to allow all students to have equal access to education.

The course began by filling out a chart that asked us what we knew about assistive technology, when we had used assistive technology/what technologies we used, and then what we wanted to know. This was very helpful in setting goals, and particular topics to look for throughout the class. The pair project that we did as a case study was also fascinating. Working with another student, I got to read a case study of a specific individual who was struggling in school due to his disability. Then, we worked together to come up with a plan for this student, and recommend assistive technologies that would be effective in providing him with the right educational setting. This assignment was helpful because it took a lot of what we had learned in the course and allowed us to make our own decisions about how to solve a real world problem.

Additionally the course involved assistive technology product reviews. I found these reviews to be really helpful because they exposed me to both software and physical products that I did not know existed. Each of the products that I reviewed were heplful in that they provided me with a good view of the landscape of assistive technologies. Also, in reading and reviewing other students' reviews, I was able to gain even more knowledge. This helped give me a breadth of solutions or possibilities in the event that I was faced with the need to help someone with disabilities learn.

Lastly, as part of this course we were asked to do a research paper that evaluated a journal article about Assistive Technology. For this assignment I chose to focus on an article that involved research about assistive technology in the workplace. As a corporate trainer, it was important for me to understand the state of assistive technology outside of just a school setting. This understanding really helped me provide assistance to my co-workers with disabilities. In fact, during the course I was aksed to help a Doctoral student with low-vision in writing his dissertation using a new screen reader software. With the help of this class, I was able to make helpful suggestions for the student and was eventually successful in setting the student up for success despite his disability.
