I know very little about assistive technology. I know the basics in terms of the fact that it is technology used to assist someone in learning or accomplishing every day tasks. I know that assistive technology is a quickly evolving area of education and an area that many schools don’t know much about even though their students could benefit significantly from it.
I have seen assistive technology used a few times as a Technology Integration Specialist in Hopkinton High School. The Special Ed department used iPods to help students with ADD or ADHD become better organized and help them focus on what they needed to accomplish. I also saw them using iPads to help non­verbal students communicate and express themselves. At Harvard Business School one of my colleagues worked with a Doctoral student who had vision problems to use dictation software so that he could write his dissertation.
