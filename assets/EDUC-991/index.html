Philosophy of Education

EDUC-991

Professor Patricia Smith

Fal 2011

Reflection

I took this course along with INST-943 during my first semester in the program. Like that course, this was a perfect class to take as the beginning of the program. While I was particularly interested in the technology side of Instructional/Educational technology, the education side was really fascinating to explore. This class provided an excellent introduction to education by presenting the various philosophies that have been developed over time. I particularly enjoyed being able to identify where I was aligned in terms of educational philosophies. While I am predominantly a progressivist, I think that at all different points throughout the program, I exhibited traits and characteristics of each of the major philosophies. Without taking this course at the outset, I would not have been able to think of how all of the other work I was doing related back to the various educational philosophies.

As I moved away from an official educational setting and into the corporate world, the philosophies that I studied in this class were still just as valuable. As part of the class we discussed what philosophies work well together and even how to tailor one's own style to help meet the needs of his/her students. I developed a good sense of how to identify the underlying philosophies of a lot of my colleagues and trainees. In doing so I was able to rely on what I knew about complimentary philosophies to help engage them and ensure they achieved as many of the possible learning objectives for the session as possible.
